CATCH ME IF YOU CAN (2002)

Directed by Steven Spielberg, this is a biographical crime film based on the life of Frank Abagnale Jr. The story follows Frank (Leonardo DiCaprio), a young con artist who successfully impersonates a Pan Am pilot, a doctor, and a legal prosecutor, all before his 19th birthday. His primary skill is check fraud, which he uses to amass millions of dollars. The film begins with Frank’s troubled family life, including his parents’ divorce, which prompts him to run away. To survive, he turns to various cons, becoming increasingly bold and sophisticated. His exploits catch the attention of FBI agent Carl Hanratty (Tom Hanks), who becomes determined to capture him. Despite Carl’s relentless pursuit, Frank manages to stay one step ahead, reveling in the chase. The cat-and-mouse game between Frank and Carl forms the crux of the film, showcasing Frank’s ingenuity and charm, as well as Carl’s dogged determination.
